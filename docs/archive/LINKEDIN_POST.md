🚀 **Excited to introduce my latest research project: the Combinatorial Optimization AI Agent!**

Have you ever stared at a complex optimization problem — scheduling, resource allocation, facility planning, logistics — and wished you could just *describe it in plain English* and get a solver-ready mathematical formulation back?

That's exactly what this agent does. 🤖

---

**What it does:**

✅ You describe your problem in natural language (e.g., *"minimize the cost of opening warehouses while meeting customer demand"*)
✅ The agent identifies the problem type from a catalog of 1,400+ combinatorial and linear programming problems
✅ It extracts the numeric parameters from your description and maps them to the right variables and constraints
✅ It returns a complete **ILP/LP formulation** — and solver-ready code in **Pyomo, Gurobi, PuLP, or GAMSPy**

---

**Under the hood:**

🔍 A semantic retrieval pipeline (TF-IDF, BM25, dense embeddings) that achieves **Schema R@1 = 0.906** on unseen queries
🔢 A downstream grounding pipeline that binds numeric mentions in natural language to schema slots — covering min/max bounds, costs, capacities, and more
📐 Structural consistency checks on generated formulations before they ever reach a solver

---

**Why does this matter?**

Operations research and combinatorial optimization are at the heart of some of the most impactful real-world decisions — supply chains, scheduling, network design, finance. Yet formulating a model correctly requires specialized expertise that most practitioners don't have. Bridging natural language and mathematical programming is a step toward making optimization accessible to everyone.
